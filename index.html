QD-MUSE: A Query-Driven Diffusion Model for Unconstrained Music Separation

Nour Abdelmoneim, Keith Ross, Gus Xia

Abstract: Music source separation (MSS) aims to isolate individual instruments from complex musical mixtures and is essential for tasks such as remixing, transcription, and audio analysis. Traditional approaches typically rely on separating a fixed set of stems using deterministic masking methods, limiting their ability to generalize to real-world mixtures with diverse instruments. In this paper, we propose QD-MUSE, a query-driven diffusion model that enables unconstrained music source separation guided by user-provided queries. By unifying timbre and melody queries within a single diffusion-based generative framework and leveraging self-supervised audio representations, our model flexibly extracts sources from arbitrary instrument combinations without relying on predefined categories. We validate QD-MUSE on the Slakh2100 dataset, demonstrating its ability to achieve high separation quality across a wide range of instruments, marking a step toward general-purpose, user-guided MSS.
